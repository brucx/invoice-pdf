Two ways to use a blank invoice

  • Print it blank and write by hand. Open the generator, leave the fields empty (or fill in just your business details), download the PDF, and print a stack. Useful for market stalls, on-site trades work, deliveries, and anywhere a laptop isn't practical.
  • Fill it out online instead. If you're at a computer anyway, typing into the form is faster than handwriting: quantities multiply out automatically, tax and discounts are calculated for you, and the total is always right. Download a finished PDF in seconds.

What a blank invoice should still include

Even a blank invoice form needs the right structure printed on it. This template lays out an invoice number and date field, From and Bill To blocks, an itemized table with quantity and unit price columns, space for tax, and a clear total-due line. Pre-fill your own business name and payment details before downloading, so every printed copy only needs the client's side written in.

Blank vs. filled: when each makes sense

A printable blank invoice shines when work happens away from a desk — a plumber finishing a job, a market vendor, a same-day delivery. An online-filled invoice PDF is better whenever accuracy and presentation matter: the math can't go wrong, the layout stays crisp, and the client receives a document that looks like it came from an established business. Since both come from the same free template, you can switch between them per job.
